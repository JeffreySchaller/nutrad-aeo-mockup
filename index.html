The Problem
Product data was never built to move this fast.

Your ERP (the system of record for items, orders, and inventory) has the item master. Your spreadsheets have the specs. Your PIM (the product catalog system) has some of the content. Your DAM (where images and brand assets live) has the assets. Your suppliers send files in different formats. Your retailers, distributors, marketplaces, and EDI (the transaction layer between trading partners) partners each want something different.

The result is predictable: delayed launches, rejected listings, manual rework, chargebacks, incomplete product pages, and teams that spend more time fixing product data than using it to grow revenue.

Three faces of the same problem

Data lives everywhere

ERP, spreadsheets, PIMs, DAMs, supplier files, portals, PDFs, and EDI feeds all hold pieces of product truth — but no one sees the whole picture.

Every channel has different rules

From retail marketplaces and distributor portals to GDSN pools, foodservice networks, healthcare channels, and B2B commerce systems, every destination requires different schemas, attributes, images, validations, certifications, and formats.

Manual work does not scale

Product teams, ecommerce teams, sales ops, and inside sales end up cleaning, mapping, enriching, and reformatting the same data again and again.

If this sounds familiar, you are not alone.

Every manufacturer and distributor we talk to is living some version of this. That is the reason nutrād exists: to take the layer between your systems and your channels off your team's plate, so your products move when commerce moves.

Industry Terms

Plain English.

A quick decoder for the acronyms used on this page.

PIM
Product Information Management. The system that holds your product catalog: descriptions, attributes, images, classifications.
ERP
Enterprise Resource Planning. The system of record for items, orders, inventory, and operations.
DAM
Digital Asset Management. Where product images, videos, and brand assets live.
EDI
Electronic Data Interchange. The transaction standard for sending orders, shipping notices, and invoices between trading partners.
GDSN
Global Data Synchronization Network. The international product data pool for grocery, foodservice, healthcare, and regulated industries.
SKU
Stock Keeping Unit. A unique product identifier used inside your business.
GTIN
Global Trade Item Number. The universal product code (UPC, EAN) that retailers, marketplaces, and trading partners use.
MDM
Master Data Management. A discipline (and a category of tools) for keeping the definitive version of business records.
